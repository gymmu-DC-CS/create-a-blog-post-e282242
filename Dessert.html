Sticky rice with mango

Ingrediens

  • 2 mangos
  • 200g stiky rice
  • 250ml coconut milk
  • 100g suger
  • a pinch of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon starch
  • 2 spoons dried mung beans

Instruction

  1. Wash the rice until the water is clear and at least 4 hours or overnight soaking.
  2. Soak mung beans in warm water.
  3. Cook rice according to instructions.
  4. Heat half of the coconut milk, add 80 g of the sugar and 1 teaspoon of salt.
  5. Pour the liquid into the still hot rice. The rice absorbs them completely in about 15 minutes.
  6. For the coconut sauce, mix cornstarch with a few drops of water, coconut milk add some salt and remaining sugar.
  7. Heat while stirring and immediately remove from the heat as soon as the sauce becomes thick.
  8. Roast mung beans in a pan over medium heat while stirring.
  9. Divide the rice and mangoes onto plates, pour coconut sauce over it and sprinkle with mung beans.

Enjoy

The story of sticky rice with mango

The origin

The origin of sticky rice with mangos dates back to the late Ayutthaya period. This was between 1351 and 1767. During this period the Thai kings became very powerful. We know that even during this time, sticky rice with mango whas a popular seet dessert thanks to a verso of that time.

Nowadays

Today sticky rice is not only a common dessert in Thailand but also in Laos and the Phillippines. In Laos it is a nationality dessert and gets served also with sesam seeds. In the philippines sticky rice is also made with puto manatype of sticky rice cake or hot chocolate. If you visite Thailand, you will see that it is a popular streetfood there.
